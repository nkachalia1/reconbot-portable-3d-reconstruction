## Texturing Fallback

Texturing the full 1.20-million-face mesh assigned all faces to source views
but crashed while building an atlas from 133,035 texture patches.

The deployment fallback was:

- Preserve the full-resolution geometry.
- Decimate a presentation copy to approximately 300,000 faces.
- Texture at half image resolution.
- Merge coplanar virtual faces.
- Disable seam leveling.
- Use fast packing and a 4096-pixel atlas.

## Visual Validation

A 42-second viewer recording was inspected at representative viewpoints across
the full rotation.

- The tape measure remains recognizable from the front, rear, side, and top.
- Object orientation and scale are visually consistent across viewpoints.
- The supporting carpet reconstructs as one continuous surface.
- Camera poses form a coherent closed trajectory around the target.
- Texture alignment is substantially better than Session 002.
- Small floating points and orange texture artifacts remain outside or near the
  main reconstruction.

Session 003 is therefore accepted as the first successful end-to-end
reconstruction. The next processing stage is geometry cleanup: crop the scene
to the desired region, remove disconnected components, and establish metric
scale from a known measured distance.

## Metric Scale

The tape-measure casing peak height was measured as 3 5/16 inches:

```text
3.3125 in = 84.1375 mm = 0.0841375 m
```

The carpet was fitted as the dominant plane. The tape measure was isolated as
the largest connected component above ten times the estimated floor noise.
Its robust 99.9th-percentile height was 0.485871 reconstruction units, yielding:

```text
scale = 0.173168491 m per reconstruction unit
```

The metric export rotates the carpet plane to `Z=0`, uses meters as the output
unit, and centers the tape measure above the XY origin. Re-estimation from the
exported mesh recovered an 84.03 mm robust height and an 84.26 mm connected
peak. This is within 0.13% of the target height. Practical accuracy remains
limited by reconstruction error and the physical measurement resolution,
approximately +/-0.8 mm when measured to the nearest 1/16 inch.
